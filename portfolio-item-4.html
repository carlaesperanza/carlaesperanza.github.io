Prizeo

Overview

Prizeo is a web application that motivates students to approach intimidating goals by giving them loyalty reward points for completing the necessary steps.


Role as lead designer

  • User Interviews, User Testing
  • Persona, User Journey map
  • Sitemap, Low-fi wireframes
  • Prototype
  • Tools used: pen and paper, Sketch, InVision, Illustrator, Coggle, Trello


    • Challenges

  • Solving a problem that I was very familiar with, it was difficult to let go of my initial assumptions on what first-year students needed (information, guidance from experienced people) and design for what most students demonstrated a need for (self-accountability)
  • To fit the 10-week time constraint I had to compromise certain features for the minimal viable product (MVP)


    • Competitors

    1)Habitica   2) Lifetick   3)Goalscape

    Insights

  • The “party” feature in Habitica that allows users to team to work towards common goals creates an element of accountability.
  • The visuals in Goalscape and Lifetick help users look at their goals from a more logical perspective that encourages taking logical approaches towards their goals.
  • None of them appeal to a diverse group of people so a large portion of the audience gets disengaged after starting.
    - Habitica is not engaging or motivating for people who don’t like videogames
    - Lifetick and goalscape have a formal tone that appeals to professionals but seems boring to students.
  • The relationship between goals and their different components are not defined or not part of the system.
    - Habitica has “Habits”, “Dailies” and “To-Dos”. All three are different types of small goals that do not relate to each other.
    - Goalscape makes it difficult to customize the importance of each sub goal and their relationship to each other is not clearly defined in the final state.
    - Lifetick only covers the management of small tasks.


    • Problem

  • Every student has a different university experience, including tackling unique challenges.
  • How can we guide a diverse group of students towards reaching their full potential?

    User Testing Results

    I conducted 2 rounds of user testing, each with 5 users. The first round showed users the digital wireframes and the second round allowed them to interact with a prototype.
  • 1st round key insights:
    • - No user expected to see a timeline as the form of tracking. They expected to see a summary of what they had inputted.
    - Users did not know to click a section of the pie chart to get to the components of the sub goal.
  • 2nd round key insights
    • - 2/5 users tried clicking on the onboarding images, thinking they would lead a corresponding section.
    - Users thought that the “goal status” was correlated to the points they had accumulated, not to the goal.
    - Asked themselves before looking at the menu how they would create a new goal.
    - Didn’t know how to go back to set the card number and ignored the “card settings” option.



    Conclusion

    To move forward with this project

  • I must figure how to ensure that people aren’t only saying that they’re completing tasks and then getting points for it
  • I want to incorporate a feature that allows them to show how many points they have gained to their friends as well as work together towards a common goal.
  • I want to include a feature that gives users points (way larger amounts) for sharing “businesses” that acted as valuable resources for working towards their goal.
